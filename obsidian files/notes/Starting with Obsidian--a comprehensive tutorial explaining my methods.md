
## Notes on the tutorial

This tutorial is ment to explain in as simple a manner as possible the way in which I use the Obsidian. My notes here will act as a reference guide for what I believe to be a very efficient usage of this app, so feel free to come back to a given note as many times as you want until you get a feel for it. I have also included multiple [[tutorials]] for additional learning and reference. I would also encourage you to reference the official [Obsidian Help](https://obsidian.md/help/) website whenever necessary.

In this tutorial, I explain all or almost all of the things necessary to use Obsidian as I use it. In addition to this, I also link to multiple YouTube tutorials for further learning. In addition to this tutorial, never hesitate to reference  for more information.

To implement the things I explain, there are a few different approaches:
1. Follow along with the notes I have created, and, as you go, implement my recommendations.
2. Still follow along with the notes, but, instead of implementing all of my recommendations one by one, simply copy all of the settings contained in this vault to your own vault. To do this, go to the `.obsidian` folder contained in this vault, and copy it into your personal vault. This will override the settings contained in your current Obsidian vault, so proceed with caution and create a duplicate if you're worried about loosing you own personal settings. 
3. The second option will apply my vault configuration to your vault, but, if you also want the structure of my vault (the `MOC.md` , my folder structure, etc.), then you can simply create a duplicate of this vault and delete my notes and add your own notes (with copy-past). 

N.B. My vault is bilingual, meaning that it accepts both English and French words, but you may change this at any time in the language settings. (This also means that I sometimes use to French spelling of words unknowingly, so please forgive any spelling errors.)
