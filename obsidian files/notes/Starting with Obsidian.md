# A comprehensive tutorial explaining my methods
## Notes on the tutorial
### About the tutorial
This tutorial is meant to explain in as simple a manner as possible the way in which I use Obsidian. I am not trying to explain *the* way to use Obsidian, but simply *my* way of using Obsidian, so I would encourage you to modify my system at any point to adapt it to *your* needs and *your* values. 

My notes here will act as a reference guide for what I believe to be a very efficient usage of this app, so feel free to come back to a given note as many times as you want until you get a feel for it. I have also included multiple [[tutorials]] for additional learning and reference. I would also encourage you to consult the official [Obsidian Help](https://obsidian.md/help/) website whenever necessary. 

N.B. This tutorial was not written to be experienced in a strictly linear fashion, so feel free to skip from section to section as you wish. 

### How to follow the tutorial
To implement the things I explain, there are a few different approaches:
1. Follow along with the notes I have created, and, as you go, implement my recommendations.
2. Still follow along with the notes, but, instead of implementing all of my recommendations one by one, simply copy all of the settings contained in this vault to your own vault. To do this, go to the `.obsidian` folder contained in this vault, and copy it into your personal vault. This will override the settings contained in your current Obsidian vault, so proceed with caution and create a duplicate if you're worried about losing your own personal settings. 
3. The second approach will apply my vault configuration to your vault, but, if you also want the structure of my vault (the `MOC.md` , my folder structure, etc.), then you can simply create a duplicate of this vault and delete my notes and add your own notes (with copy-paste). 

N.B. My vault is bilingual, meaning that it accepts both English and French words, but you may change this at any time in the language settings. (This also means that I sometimes use the French spelling of words unknowingly, so please forgive any spelling errors.)


### My thoughts on the tutorial as it is now


##### Self deprecation time!
This tutorial took me a bit longer than expected, but, even so, there are many things I believe that I could improve, not to mention the things that I haven't even finished. On top of the fact that my wording is often sluggish and my sentences too long, there is a lot of general polish that the tutorial still needs, so I am committed to it as a long term project. (I frankly don't even want to know how many typos are in the many thousands of words I used to yap about Obsidian.)

##### This tutorial moving forwards--what's going to change

This tutorial is a guide to the methods I have been using for the past few months and which I used to create a multi-thousand note vault; however, I plan on slightly modifying some of my practices when it comes to organizing notes with [[metadata]]. Of course, when I do finally decide on my new methods, I will be writing about them here, so you won't have to worry about missing out on them. But in any case, the things I have already explained in this tutorial are largely sufficient for creating a solid base for your vault, and refactoring after the fact is never very hard (especially if you are willing to allow an AI to do some of the *zombie* work for you.) In any case, as you discover this app for yourself, you will surely find what works for you, and my eccentric methods will be of no interest to you, I'm sure. 

N.B. In this vault, there is a *classes* folder, but you can delete or ignore it because it is not used in my current method.
