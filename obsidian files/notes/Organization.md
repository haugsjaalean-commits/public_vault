
## The philosophy of organization

This is hands down the most important section in the tutorial. The way you choose to organize your vault will decide everything. Given this, the stage where we try to decide how to organize our vault is often very overwhelming (at least for me), and it's very easy to get distracted by things that don't matter, or simply never start because you're always trying to decide what the best way to move forwards is; in fact, in [[The Thinking Person's Set Up (FULL SET UP)]], Vicky Zhao says that only 1% of our time in Obsidian should be spent thinking about how to go about organizing our notes. I think that, for someone like me, this number is too small and would only hurt my process; however, I do think that it's important to embrace a certain *Tao of organization*, where we realize that we can neither spend all of our time thinking about organizing, nor none of our time thinking about it. I believe it is by proceeding like this that we will get the most value out of a tool like Obsidian, as imperfect as it may be. 


## How can we do organization? 

In order to organize, we must first know what it means to organize. I have thought far too much about this very question, but I think that everyone has a pretty good intuition of what organization is without ever needing to ponder it. If you would like to ponder it, consider reading [[The theory of information]], my personal collection of thoughts on organization. 

Once we believe we understand what it means to organize, we may start organizing. The only problem is that Obsidian is not a perfect tool for this task.
